Judging Criteria

1. Innovation & Creativity (20 points)

Does the project introduce a unique idea or an original solution? Does it creatively address a real-world problem?

2. Technical Complexity (20 points)

How technically impressive is the project? Does it demonstrate strong coding, data handling, or system design? Is it appropriately challenging given the time frame?

3. Impact & Relevance (20 points)

Does the project solve a meaningful problem? Could it make a real-world difference?

4. User Experience & Design (15 points)

Is the project intuitive and user-friendly? Does it have a polished design and smooth user flow? Is accessibility considered?

5. Functionality & Completion (15 points)

Does the project work as intended? Is there a functional demo? (Even if incomplete, can the core idea be demonstrated?) How well was the idea executed given the time constraints?

6. Presentation & Pitch (10 points)

Was the team able to clearly explain their project, its impact, and how it works? Was the pitch engaging and well-structured? Did they effectively answer judges’ questions?

Bonus: Community/Cultural Integration (+5 points)

Does the project incorporate elements that celebrate or uplift Latino culture, heritage, or community challenges?

SolHacks 2026 Official Rules

1. Team Formation

Teams must have 1 to 4 members. Participants can form teams before the event or during the team formation session. All team members must be officially registered for SolHacks.

2. Project Requirements

Projects must be built entirely during the hackathon (no pre-existing projects allowed). Teams may use publicly available libraries, APIs, and frameworks, but all significant code must be written during SolHacks. Any AI-generated code (e.g., ChatGPT, Copilot) must be properly reviewed and understood by the team. All projects must be safe, legal, and ethical (no offensive, harmful, or discriminatory content).

3. Hacking Period

Hacking officially begins at March 28th, 2026 at 10AM and ends at March 29th, 2026 at 10AM. Teams must submit their projects before the deadline—late submissions may not be considered. Only work done within the hackathon timeframe will be judged.

4. Code of Conduct

All participants must follow the MLH Code of Conduct. Harassment, discrimination, or disruptive behavior will not be tolerated. Be respectful of others, whether online or in person. If you experience or witness misconduct, please report it to the organizing team.

5. Judging & Prizes

Projects will be judged based on innovation, technical complexity, impact, design, functionality, and presentation. Judges' decisions are final. Prizes will be awarded at the closing ceremony, and teams must be present to receive them.

6. Venue & Logistics

Sleeping areas will be designated, please respect others’ space. Keep all areas clean and follow venue rules.

7. Use of AI & External Tools

AI tools like ChatGPT and GitHub Copilot are allowed for assistance but cannot generate entire projects. AI-generated content must be properly credited.

8. Mentorship & Help

Mentors will be available throughout the event. Feel free to ask for help! Teams are encouraged to collaborate and learn, but copying another team’s work is not allowed.

9. Submissions

Projects must be submitted via Devpost by March 29th, 2026 at 10AM. Each submission should include:

  • A brief project description
  • A 2-minute demo video
  • A link to the source code (if applicable)

10. Have Fun & Hack Responsibly!

SolHacks is about learning, networking, and creating something awesome. Enjoy the process!
